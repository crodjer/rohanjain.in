A new twitter policy

January 5, 2017 | social-media

I am fairly active on twitter. It is a very convenient place to register interesting thoughts and ideas. I actually like to revisit my twitter history frequently and each of the tweets function as a photograph to what my state of the mind, the context was at the time I published the tweet. Hence, I personally can recover most of the idea.

But 140 characters aren’t generally enough to convey the context plus the idea, leaving an immense amount of room for misunderstanding. To someone else reading the tweets, it mostly would be meaningless or worse, they’d end up misinterpreting it.

So, I have decided I’ll tweet less and try to keep them more accessible. There is no point in inflicting gratuitous ideas to public who most likely uses twitter as a news aggregator.

As far as keeping snapshots of my states of mind at various points of history is concerned, I am yet to figure that out. Maybe a good old physical notebook would do the job.
