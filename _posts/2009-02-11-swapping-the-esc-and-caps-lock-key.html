Well, the first question that comes to mind after getting a hang of the 'almighty VIM' is "Is there a way to swap 'esc' key with something near the home row?".Aye! there is. Swap the 'esc' and 'caps lock' key. Here is how:

  1. Fire up Vim to create an (say 'keymap') ASCII plain text file (vim keymap) with the content:
    remove Lock = Caps_Lock
    keysym Escape = Caps_Lock
    keysym Caps_Lock = Escape
    add Lock = Caps_Lock

  2. now execute the command:
    xmodmap "filename", obviously without the quotes, and you are done.

The above applies to all distros of linux. Personally I have tested it on Debian. And yes, the change is global.
