Learning German

January 8, 2017 | german, languages, reading

There are far too many famous scientists, philosophers, authors that I admire who published their work in German. So, I am considering to put in some serious effort in learning the language and take on reading some of these famous works without any English translations.

Mostly likely, I’ll start with re-reading Wittgenstein’s, Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus in german, specifically because of the unique style of writing. It will make comparing (and hence learning) with the translations easier.

Other popular authors I’d like to read are:

  • Einstein
  • Wittgenstein
  • Kant (too ambitious?)
  • Neitzche
  • Johann Wolfgang von Goethe

On a more practicle side, learning multiple languages is considered to be good for brain development. Moreover, its a good to have professionally.
